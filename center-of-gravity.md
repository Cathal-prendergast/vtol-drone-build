# Centre of Gravity

## What is Center of Gravity?

The Center of Gravity of an object is the point on it where the weight is evenly distributed on all sides of the object. It is where the weight of the object appears to act.
The aircraft rotates around its centre of gravity, making its position critical for stability and control.

---

## Importance of CG Location

The position of the CG has a major effect on flight behaviour:

- A forward CG increases stability but makes the aircraft less responsive.
- A rear CG reduces stability and can make the aircraft difficult to control.
- A correctly positioned CG ensures stable a flight.

---

## Target CG Position

The target centre of gravity is located at 30% of the wing chord from the leading edge.

Wing chord = 0.23583 m  
Target CG ≈ 0.071 m from leading edge

---

## Achieving Correct CG

The CG is adjusted through component placement:

- Battery position (main adjustment method)
- Distribution of electronics and flight controller
- Symmetrical placement of motors and ESCs

The final CG is verified by physically balancing the aircraft during assembly.

---

**Correct CG placement is critical for:**

- Stable forward flight
- Smooth VTOL-to-forward transition
- Safe take-off and landing behaviour

An incorrect CG position can make the aircraft unstable.
